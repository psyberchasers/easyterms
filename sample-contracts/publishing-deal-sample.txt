EXCLUSIVE SONGWRITER AND CO-PUBLISHING AGREEMENT

This Exclusive Songwriter and Co-Publishing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of February 1, 2024, by and between:

WRITER: Jasmine "J-Melody" Thompson, residing at 789 Harmony Street, Nashville, TN 37203 ("Writer")

AND

PUBLISHER: Hitmaker Publishing Group, Inc., a Tennessee corporation with offices at 456 Music Row, Nashville, TN 37203 ("Publisher")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Writer is a talented songwriter who desires to have her musical compositions published and exploited; and

WHEREAS, Publisher is engaged in the music publishing business and desires to acquire rights in Writer's musical compositions;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: TERM

1.1 The initial term shall be two (2) years from the Effective Date ("Initial Term").

1.2 Publisher shall have three (3) options to extend for additional one (1) year periods each ("Option Terms").

1.3 The Initial Term and any Option Terms are collectively the "Term."

ARTICLE 2: GRANT OF RIGHTS

2.1 Copyright Assignment. Writer hereby assigns, transfers, and conveys to Publisher an undivided fifty percent (50%) of all right, title, and interest in and to all musical compositions written by Writer during the Term ("Compositions"), including but not limited to:

    (a) Fifty percent (50%) of all copyrights and renewals thereof
    (b) Fifty percent (50%) of all income derived from the Compositions

2.2 Administration Rights. Writer grants Publisher the exclusive right to administer one hundred percent (100%) of each Composition worldwide, including the right to:

    (a) Register copyrights in Publisher's name
    (b) Issue licenses for mechanical reproduction, synchronization, and all other uses
    (c) Collect all income from any source
    (d) Take legal action to protect the Compositions

2.3 Territory. The rights granted herein shall apply throughout the world and universe, now known or hereafter devised.

ARTICLE 3: ADVANCES

3.1 Signing Advance. Upon execution, Publisher shall pay Writer Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000).

3.2 Annual Advances. Publisher shall pay Writer the following advances:

    (a) Year 1: $50,000 (signing advance)
    (b) Year 2: $65,000
    (c) Option Year 1: $80,000
    (d) Option Year 2: $100,000
    (e) Option Year 3: $125,000

3.3 Recoupment. All advances are recoupable from Writer's share of income.

ARTICLE 4: DELIVERY COMMITMENT

4.1 Minimum Delivery. Writer agrees to deliver to Publisher:

    (a) Initial Term: A minimum of twenty (20) wholly-written Compositions per year, or forty (40) co-written Compositions (with Writer's share equaling at least 50%)
    (b) Option Terms: Same commitment as Initial Term

4.2 Commercial Releases. At least ten (10) Compositions per year must be commercially released on a major or significant independent label.

ARTICLE 5: ROYALTIES AND INCOME DIVISION

5.1 Publisher's Share. Publisher shall retain:

    (a) Fifty percent (50%) of all gross receipts from mechanical royalties
    (b) Fifty percent (50%) of all gross receipts from synchronization fees
    (c) Fifty percent (50%) of all gross receipts from print income
    (d) Fifty percent (50%) of all other income

5.2 Writer's Share. Writer shall receive:

    (a) Fifty percent (50%) of all gross receipts described above
    (b) One hundred percent (100%) of Writer's performance royalties collected by PROs

5.3 Administration Fee. Publisher may deduct an administration fee of ten percent (10%) from gross receipts before computing the income split.

5.4 Co-Writer Splits. If a Composition is co-written, Writer's share shall be reduced proportionately based on Writer's actual contribution.

ARTICLE 6: SYNCHRONIZATION LICENSES

6.1 Approval Rights. Writer shall have approval rights over synchronization licenses for the following:

    (a) Political advertisements or campaigns
    (b) Adult content
    (c) Tobacco or firearms advertising
    (d) Any use that could reasonably be considered defamatory

6.2 Other Sync Uses. For all other synchronization uses, Publisher shall have sole discretion, provided that Publisher uses commercially reasonable efforts to obtain fair market value.

6.3 Sync Fee Minimums. Publisher agrees not to license any Composition for synchronization use for less than:

    (a) Major motion picture: $25,000
    (b) Network television: $10,000
    (c) Streaming series: $7,500
    (d) Advertising (national): $50,000
    (e) Advertising (regional): $15,000

ARTICLE 7: ACCOUNTING

7.1 Statements. Publisher shall render quarterly accounting statements within sixty (60) days after each calendar quarter, accompanied by payment of amounts due.

7.2 Audit Rights. Writer may audit Publisher's books once per year with thirty (30) days notice. If discrepancies exceed five percent (5%), Publisher shall pay audit costs.

ARTICLE 8: CREATIVE SERVICES

8.1 Demo Recordings. Publisher shall provide Writer with a demo recording budget of up to $25,000 per year for the creation of demonstration recordings.

8.2 Co-Writing Sessions. Publisher shall use reasonable efforts to arrange co-writing sessions with other successful songwriters in Publisher's roster.

8.3 Pitching Services. Publisher shall actively pitch Writer's Compositions to recording artists, record labels, film/TV music supervisors, and advertising agencies.

ARTICLE 9: WRITER WARRANTIES

9.1 Writer represents and warrants that:

    (a) Writer has full authority to enter into this Agreement
    (b) All Compositions shall be original and not infringe any third party rights
    (c) Writer is not subject to any conflicting agreements
    (d) Writer shall be a member in good standing of ASCAP, BMI, or SESAC

ARTICLE 10: REVERSION

10.1 Unrecouped Reversion. If any Composition has not been commercially exploited within three (3) years of delivery and Writer's account remains unrecouped, Writer may request reversion of such Composition, and Publisher shall reassign Writer's share upon written request.

10.2 Catalog Reversion. If at any time Writer's account becomes fully recouped and remains recouped for two (2) consecutive accounting periods, Writer may purchase Publisher's share of the catalog at fair market value, to be determined by mutual agreement or independent appraisal.

ARTICLE 11: EXCLUSIVITY

11.1 During the Term, Writer shall render exclusive songwriter services to Publisher. Writer shall not write, compose, or create any musical composition for any other party without Publisher's prior written consent.

11.2 Exception: Writer may collaborate on compositions for specific recording projects by major label artists, provided that Publisher receives its full share of such compositions.

ARTICLE 12: RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

12.1 Following expiration of the Term, if Writer receives a bona fide offer from a third party for a publishing agreement, Writer shall notify Publisher in writing, and Publisher shall have thirty (30) days to match such offer.

ARTICLE 13: TERMINATION

13.1 Either party may terminate upon material breach by the other party that remains uncured for thirty (30) days after written notice.

13.2 Upon termination, all rights in Compositions delivered prior to termination shall remain with Publisher pursuant to the terms hereof.

ARTICLE 14: MISCELLANEOUS

14.1 This Agreement shall be governed by Tennessee law.

14.2 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

14.3 Publisher may assign this Agreement; Writer may not assign without consent.
