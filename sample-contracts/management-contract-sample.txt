ARTIST MANAGEMENT AGREEMENT

This Artist Management Agreement ("Agreement") is made effective as of April 1, 2024, by and between:

ARTIST: The Neon Waves, a musical group consisting of:
- Alex Rivera (vocals/guitar)
- Sam Chen (bass)  
- Jordan Williams (drums)
- Taylor Brooks (keyboards)
(collectively referred to as "Artist")

AND

MANAGER: Amplify Artist Management, LLC, a California limited liability company, located at 2000 Avenue of the Stars, Suite 1500, Los Angeles, CA 90067 ("Manager")

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Artist is a musical performing and recording group seeking professional management; and

WHEREAS, Manager is in the business of managing musical artists and has the expertise and industry contacts to advance Artist's career;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1: ENGAGEMENT AND TERM

1.1 Engagement. Artist hereby engages Manager as Artist's sole and exclusive personal manager throughout the world.

1.2 Term. The initial term shall be three (3) years from the Effective Date ("Initial Term").

1.3 Options. Manager shall have two (2) options to extend for additional two (2) year periods each, exercisable by written notice at least sixty (60) days before expiration of the then-current term.

1.4 Performance Benchmarks. Manager's options are contingent upon Artist achieving at least one of the following during each contract period:

    (a) Gross earnings of at least $200,000 per year
    (b) Securing a major label recording agreement
    (c) Headlining venues of 1,000+ capacity
    (d) Achieving 1,000,000 cumulative streams on major platforms

ARTICLE 2: MANAGER'S SERVICES

2.1 Manager agrees to perform the following services:

    (a) Advise and counsel Artist in all matters relating to Artist's career
    (b) Seek and negotiate recording, publishing, merchandising, and touring opportunities
    (c) Coordinate with booking agents, record labels, publishers, and other industry professionals
    (d) Advise on marketing, branding, and public relations strategies
    (e) Assist in budgeting and financial planning for Artist's career
    (f) Attend important performances and meetings when reasonably requested
    (g) Be reasonably available to consult with Artist

2.2 Manager shall use reasonable efforts and devote reasonable time to Artist's career, but Manager may represent other artists whose careers do not directly conflict with Artist.

ARTICLE 3: COMPENSATION

3.1 Commission Rate. Artist shall pay Manager a commission equal to:

    (a) Twenty percent (20%) of Gross Earnings during the Term
    (b) Fifteen percent (15%) of Gross Earnings from agreements substantially negotiated during the Term but paid after expiration ("Sunset Period")

3.2 Sunset Period. The Sunset Period and reduced commission rate shall apply as follows:

    (a) Years 1-2 after Term: 15% commission
    (b) Years 3-4 after Term: 10% commission
    (c) Year 5 after Term: 5% commission
    (d) Thereafter: 0% commission

3.3 Definition of Gross Earnings. "Gross Earnings" means all gross monies or other consideration received by or credited to Artist from Artist's activities in the entertainment industry, including but not limited to:

    (a) Recording agreements and record sales/streaming
    (b) Live performances and touring
    (c) Music publishing and songwriting
    (d) Merchandising
    (e) Sponsorships and endorsements
    (f) Film, television, and commercial appearances
    (g) Social media and digital content revenue
    (h) Any other entertainment-related income

3.4 Exclusions from Gross Earnings. The following shall NOT be commissionable:

    (a) Sound recording costs paid directly by a record label
    (b) Tour support paid directly by a record label
    (c) Bona fide reimbursement of travel and living expenses
    (d) Awards, gifts, or prizes
    (e) Earnings from non-entertainment employment

3.5 Payment. All Gross Earnings shall be paid to Manager or Manager's designated business manager, who shall deduct Manager's commission and remit the balance to Artist within fifteen (15) days.

ARTICLE 4: ARTIST'S OBLIGATIONS

4.1 Artist agrees to:

    (a) Pursue Artist's career diligently and professionally
    (b) Consult with Manager regarding all professional matters
    (c) Refer all inquiries and offers to Manager
    (d) Not enter into agreements without Manager's review and input
    (e) Maintain professional conduct and protect Artist's reputation
    (f) Provide Manager with accurate financial information

4.2 Artist shall not engage any other personal manager during the Term.

ARTICLE 5: AUTHORITY AND APPROVALS

5.1 Manager Authority. Manager is authorized to:

    (a) Negotiate agreements on Artist's behalf (subject to Artist approval)
    (b) Collect and receive payments on Artist's behalf
    (c) Approve publicity materials and press releases
    (d) Engage publicists, attorneys, and other professionals on Artist's behalf

5.2 Artist Approval Required. The following require Artist's prior written approval:

    (a) All agreements and contracts
    (b) Expenditures exceeding $5,000
    (c) Selection of booking agents, attorneys, and business managers
    (d) Major creative decisions (album titles, cover art, etc.)
    (e) Endorsement or sponsorship deals

5.3 Power of Attorney. Artist grants Manager a limited power of attorney to execute documents and conduct business on Artist's behalf, but only with respect to matters previously approved by Artist.

ARTICLE 6: ACCOUNTING

6.1 Manager shall maintain accurate books and records of all financial transactions.

6.2 Manager shall provide monthly statements of all Gross Earnings and commission deductions.

6.3 Artist may audit Manager's books once per year with thirty (30) days notice.

ARTICLE 7: EXPENSES

7.1 Manager shall be responsible for Manager's ordinary office overhead and administrative costs.

7.2 Artist shall reimburse Manager for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred on Artist's behalf, including:

    (a) Travel expenses when accompanying Artist
    (b) Long-distance communications
    (c) Promotional materials and showcases
    (d) Legal fees (with prior approval)

7.3 Expenses exceeding $1,000 require Artist's prior approval.

ARTICLE 8: KEY MAN PROVISION

8.1 This Agreement is entered into based on Artist's desire to be personally managed by Marcus Thompson ("Key Man").

8.2 If Key Man ceases to be actively involved in managing Artist for any reason, Artist may terminate this Agreement upon thirty (30) days written notice.

ARTICLE 9: WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

9.1 Artist warrants that:

    (a) Artist has the right to enter into this Agreement
    (b) All members of the group consent to this Agreement
    (c) Artist is not subject to any conflicting management agreements

9.2 Manager warrants that:

    (a) Manager has the right to enter into this Agreement
    (b) Manager has the experience and relationships to perform services hereunder
    (c) Manager maintains appropriate professional liability insurance

ARTICLE 10: TERMINATION

10.1 Either party may terminate upon:

    (a) Material breach by the other party that remains uncured for thirty (30) days
    (b) Bankruptcy or insolvency of the other party
    (c) Death or permanent disability of Key Man (Artist's option)

10.2 Artist may terminate if Manager fails to secure any of the following within eighteen (18) months:

    (a) A bona fide recording agreement
    (b) A national booking agency agreement
    (c) Gross Earnings exceeding $75,000 in any twelve-month period

10.3 Upon termination, Manager's commission rights shall continue per the Sunset provisions for agreements substantially negotiated during the Term.

ARTICLE 11: INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 Artist shall indemnify Manager against claims arising from Artist's breach of this Agreement or Artist's professional activities.

11.2 Manager shall indemnify Artist against claims arising from Manager's breach of this Agreement or Manager's negligence.

ARTICLE 12: MISCELLANEOUS

12.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by California law.

12.2 Disputes. Any disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration in Los Angeles, California.

12.3 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

12.4 Assignment. Neither party may assign without the other's written consent, except Manager may assign to an affiliate.

12.5 Group Provisions. If any member leaves the group, this Agreement shall continue with remaining members. New members shall be bound by this Agreement upon joining.
